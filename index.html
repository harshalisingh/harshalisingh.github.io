Data for Development: The analysis and Visualization of Mobile phone data

Orange Telecom's challenge for socio-economic developement of Ivory Coast
January 2013 - June 2013

The Orange “Data for Development” - D4D - was an open data challenge, encouraging research teams around the world to use four datasets of anonymous call patterns of Orange's Ivory Coast subsidiary, to help address society development questions in novel ways by analysing the datasets and finding interesting patterns. I as a part of a team of 3 took part in the challenge.

We were given access to four mobile phone datasets. The datasets were based on anonymized Call Detail Records (CDR) of phone calls between five million of Orange's customers in Ivory Coast between December 1, 2011 and April 28, 2012.

The data was analysed by populating the HBase running on a 12 node Hadoop cluster and queried using Hive. An interactive visualization was created using D3.js after the data was verified and compared with other data sources.

This project resulted in us submitting a paper On the Analysis and Visualization of Anonymized Call Detail Records to Advanced Computing and Communications Society (ADCOM 2013).
